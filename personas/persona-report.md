# Workflow Stage: Execution (Front-line)

---

## Persona: Aisha Begum – Healthcare Assistant (HCA)

**Role:** Healthcare Assistant (Band 2, Acute Medicine)
**Department:** Emergency Department (ED) Overflow
**Reports to:** Senior Staff Nurse (ED)

**Background:**
Aisha, 27, has worked as an HCA in the NHS for 4 years, mostly in acute and emergency settings. She is known for her calm manner and ability to build rapport with anxious patients. Aisha often covers overflow areas and temporary wards, where she juggles multiple patients and is frequently called to help with urgent tasks.

**Goals & Outcomes:**
- Ensure all patients in her area have up-to-date vital signs recorded
- Spot early signs of deterioration and escalate promptly
- Track her own completion of observation rounds
- Wants the system to help her prioritise which patients need obs next

**Wants, Needs & Expectations:**
- Needs a quick, mobile-friendly way to enter vitals
- Relies on clear prompts for overdue/missing observations
- Prefers minimal typing and clear patient lists
- Needs basic training and visual guides
- Communicates mostly face-to-face or via walkie-talkie

**Pain Points & Unmet Needs:**
- Frustrated by paper obs charts getting lost or delayed
- Finds it hard to keep track of patients in non-standard areas
- Wishes for reminders and a single list of all her patients
- Manual data entry is time-consuming

**Additional Context:**
- Typical day: 12-hour shift, 20+ patients, constant interruptions
- Comfortable with basic digital tools (e.g., e-obs, WhatsApp)
- Key relationships: Staff nurses, patients, porters
- No decision-making authority but can escalate concerns
- Must follow infection control and documentation standards
- Juggles high workload, often skips breaks
- Fluent in English and Bengali

- [NHS Agenda for Change Band 2 HCA](https://www.healthcareers.nhs.uk/explore-roles/clinical-support-staff/roles-clinical-support-staff/healthcare-assistant)
- [NHS Staff Survey 2023](https://www.nhsstaffsurveys.com/)
